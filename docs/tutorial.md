# Tutorial: Memento

## Overview

This is Memento. We built this for students and networking professionals to save time and make connections more meaningful and less random. Our solution helps you figure out who you should talk to, giving you relevant information about the person before you can even say hello. With the help of the Mentra glasses and our app, you can make networking more deliberate and leave less to chance, which ensures that you have meaningful conversations with meaningful people.

## Prerequisites

The only prerequisites that you must have are:

- **Mentra glasses** — any supported model
- **Mentra app** — downloaded on your mobile phone

With just these two devices, not only will you be able to save time when going to networking events, but you will be able to go to such events with much more confidence and be able to make real connections that can potentially change the trajectory of your career.

## Setup / Installation

### Step 1: Download the Mentra App

Download the Mentra app on your mobile phone from your device's app store.

### Step 2: Pair Your Glasses

1. Open the Mentra app on your phone.
2. Make sure your Mentra glasses are **turned on**.
3. Ensure **Bluetooth** and **Wi-Fi** are enabled on your phone.
4. Tap **Pair Glasses** in the app.
5. Select the **model** of your glasses when prompted.
6. A **Pair** button will appear — tap it.
7. The app will load to a new screen confirming that your glasses are connected.

### Step 3: Install Memento on Your Glasses

1. After your glasses are connected, go to the **Interactive Apps** tab in the Mentra app.
2. Search for **Memento**.
3. Download and open it.

You are now ready to begin using Memento.

## First Workflow

### Signing Up

The first page will be a sign-up/login page. Since it is your first time, you will have to sign up. This can be done in two ways:

- **Email sign-up** — create an account with your email and a password.
- **Google sign-in** — use your Google account for a faster setup.

The choice is entirely yours.

### Building Your Profile

After you sign up, the next page is where you build your profile. You have two options:

- **LinkedIn import** — input your LinkedIn username to pull in your professional information.
- **Resume upload** — upload a PDF or DOCX resume to automatically extract your details.

Once you have done so, the app will give you a **completeness score** that shows how complete your profile is and what information is missing. This is also where you can **manually review your profile** to ensure that the information is accurate. When you are happy with your information, hit **Continue**.

### Face Enrollment

The next page will prompt you to:

1. **Upload or take a photo of your face** — you can either manually take a photo or upload an existing photo, both options are available.
2. **Agree to the terms and conditions** — this means you are choosing to opt into our facial recognition database so that other attendees can recognize you at events.

When you are done with this step, the app will have you **review your profile one last time** to make sure you are happy with how it looks.

### Browsing Events

After completing your profile, you will be taken to the **Events page** where you can:

- Browse through events in your local area by using the **search bar**.
- Use **filters** to help you better find what you are looking for.

If an event is active and you have registered for it, click on the event to proceed to the recognition page.

### Recognition

Once you are on the recognition page:

1. There will be a **bubble in the top-left corner** that tells you whether the glasses are recognizing or not.
2. You can **toggle recognition** using the button on the left side of the glasses.
3. When recognition is active and you look at someone, their image will show up on the recognition page along with a **short bio** and a **similarity score**.
4. As you look around the room, more and more people will appear on this page.

### Viewing a Profile

If you are interested in someone:

1. **Tap on their profile** on the recognition page.
2. This will take you to a **detailed profile page** with much more information about the person, including a **conversation starter** to help you break the ice.
3. If you really like this person, you can **heart their profile** and go back to it later — for up to **48 hours** after the event.

Once you are satisfied with the information, hit **Back** and continue scanning people around the room. This helps you decide who to talk to at events and ensures you do not waste your time with meaningless conversations.

## Expected Results

- Profiles should show up on the recognition page as long as the bubble in the top-left corner says **Recognizing**.
- When you tap on a profile, detailed information about the person should appear.
- If any of this is not working, refer to the troubleshooting steps below.

## Troubleshooting

### Profile import issues

- When signing up with LinkedIn, ensure that you are using the **correct LinkedIn username** — double-check the spelling.
- When uploading a resume, use your **most recent resume** to ensure the information is up to date.

### Glasses pairing issues

- Make sure **Bluetooth** and **Wi-Fi** are enabled on your phone.
- Ensure the **most recent version** of the Mentra app is downloaded.
- Make sure your glasses are **powered on** before attempting to pair.

### No profiles showing up on the recognition page

- Confirm that you are **connected to Wi-Fi** and **Bluetooth is on**.
- Verify that your glasses are **paired with the app**.
- Check that the recognition bubble is toggled **on** — if it is not, press the button on the **left side of the glasses** to enable recognition.
